To better understand the public health crisis of overdose deaths facing B.C., the Vancouver Sun is following the latest data published by the B.C. Coroners’ Service and other government agencies, which publish new information monthly. Scroll for more information or jump to a section using the menu, above.

Where the deaths are occuring

Overdose deaths have touched every corner of the province. While most deaths occur in Vancouver, some of the highest rates of death, for both men and women, are in the Northern and Interior parts of the province.

The drugs that are killing people

Fentanyl is regularly detected in over 80 per cent of overdose deaths. During the pandemic, overdoses involving extreme concentrations of fentanyl or those involving benzodiazepines – which reduce the efficacy of Naloxone – increased significantly.

Who’s being killed

Males aged 30-60 face the highest risk, on average, and most overdoses occur in the home. Northern B.C. has the highest rate of overdose deaths among women, though, and in Vancouver, most overdoses occur in SROs or other hotel settings.
